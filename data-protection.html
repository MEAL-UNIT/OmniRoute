SURAKKHA Project

Data Protection

How we handle the personal information people share with us — separate from, but related to, our safeguarding commitments.

Along with keeping people physically and emotionally safe, we're careful with the personal information people share with us — names, contact details, photos, and case details collected through this project's forms and activities.

Our Principles

  • We collect only what's needed for the purpose at hand — nothing extra "just in case."
  • Photos, videos, and personal stories are only used with documented, informed consent — see the Consent Form.
  • Sensitive records (incident reports, case details) are shared only with people who need them to respond appropriately.
  • People can withdraw consent at any time — see the Consent Form for how that works.
  • Personal data is kept only as long as it's needed for the project, then removed.

Data Sharing

Project data is never shared outside the immediate team casually. Any time it needs to go to a donor, partner, or other third party, it follows an approval process:

1

State the purpose

What's being shared, with whom, and why — clearly written down before anything moves.

2

Approval from the Project Lead

No data leaves the team without the Project Lead's sign-off on that stated purpose.

3

Verified by the MEAL Focal Person

The MEAL focal checks the sharing is appropriate and consistent with what people actually consented to.

4

Share the minimum necessary

Only the specific data needed for that stated purpose — not the full record by default.

5

Keep a record

The request and the approval are documented, so there's a clear trail of who approved what, and why.
