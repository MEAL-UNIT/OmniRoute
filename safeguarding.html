SURAKKHA Project

Safeguarding & Protection

Our shared commitment to keeping children, adolescents, and communities safe — and how to raise a concern if you ever need to.

Safeguarding means the responsibility every organisation and individual has to prevent harm — abuse, exploitation, and neglect — from happening to the people we work with and for, especially children. On SURAKKHA, that responsibility belongs to everyone: staff, volunteers, partners, and the communities we serve.

Our Core Principles

🧒

Best Interest of the Child

Every decision and action puts the safety and wellbeing of children first.

🛡️

Do No Harm

Our presence and activities should never put anyone at greater risk.

🔒

Confidentiality

Information about a concern is shared only with those who need to know, to protect everyone involved.

⚖️

Non-Discrimination

Every concern is taken seriously regardless of who raises it or who it concerns.

🚫

Zero Tolerance

Abuse, exploitation, and harassment are never acceptable, from anyone, under any circumstance.

📋

Accountability

Every report is followed up, and the person raising it is kept informed appropriately.

Code of Conduct — Key Commitments

  • All staff, volunteers, and partners complete safeguarding and PSEA training before working with communities.
  • No staff member may be alone with a child in an unsupervised, closed setting.
  • Photos or stories involving children are only used with documented, informed consent.
  • Any exchange of money, gifts, or favours in return for assistance or access is strictly prohibited.
  • Every team member is expected — and protected — when reporting a genuine concern in good faith.

Data Protection

How we handle personal information — names, contact details, photos, and case details — is covered separately, in our Data Protection page.

How to Raise a Concern

Safeguarding / PSEA Incident

For any actual or suspected incident of abuse or exploitation.

General Incident

Security, accident, fire, or other non-safeguarding incidents.

Immediate Information Share

Send a quick notification while you gather full details.

This page intentionally contains only general safeguarding information and principles — no details about specific cases, incidents, or individuals are ever published here, in keeping with FIVDB's commitment to confidentiality.
